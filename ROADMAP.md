# Curriculum roadmap

Ordered by learner impact: safety and connection first, then services.
Global English; principles over vendor button-paths. One lesson at a time,
quality over volume. (Owner of this file: Iris, edu-ceo.)

## Published

1. Getting to know your smartphone — touches, home screen, the rescue move.
2. Spotting scams: the golden rules — the pattern that defeats most scams.
3. Video calls with your family — receiving, calling, mute, freezes.

## Next (in order)

4. Sending photos to family — take, find, share; what "the cloud" means.
5. Messaging basics — texts vs internet messages, groups, voice notes.
6. Passwords without tears — what makes one strong, writing them down
   sensibly, why one password everywhere is the real danger.
7. Paying safely online — recognizing a secure checkout, cards vs
   transfers, why gift cards are never payment.
8. Using government services online — the general shape of official
   portals, spotting fake ones.
9. Email confidently — reading, replying, attachments, spotting phishing
   (builds on the scams lesson).
10. When something goes wrong — restarting, storage-full, "have you tried
    turning it off and on" explained with dignity.

## Later themes

Health appointments online · maps and getting around · news and spotting
misinformation · tablets vs phones · helping a friend learn (teaching the
teachers) · translations (first candidate: Hindi).

## Standing quality bar

Every lesson: passes the QA checklist in the operating manual, ends with
"Try it yourself" + a printable summary, and reads with dignity — we never
write down to our learners.
