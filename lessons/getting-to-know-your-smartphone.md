# Getting to know your smartphone

**Who this is for:** complete beginners. If you already send messages comfortably, start with the scams lesson instead.  
**Time:** 20–30 minutes, no rush.  
**You need:** your phone.

First, the most important thing in this whole lesson:

**You cannot break your phone by touching the screen.** Whatever happens, there is always a way back. The worst case is that something confusing appears — and this lesson shows you how to get home from anywhere.

---

## The three touches

Your phone understands a few simple touches. Practice each one now — on any screen, it does not matter which.

1. **Tap** — touch the screen once, lightly and briefly, like pressing a doorbell. This is how you choose things. If nothing happens, tap once more a little more firmly. (No need to press hard — the screen feels touch, not force.)
2. **Swipe** — place your finger on the screen and slide it, like turning a page. Sliding up or down moves through long pages; left or right changes screens.
3. **Press and hold** — touch the screen and keep your finger still for two seconds. This opens extra choices, like a menu. If a menu you did not want appears, tap any empty space and it goes away.

## Your home screen

The **home screen** (the phone's front page, with a grid of small pictures) is your starting point. Each small picture is an **app** (one program that does one job — the camera app takes photos, the phone app makes calls).

**The one move that always rescues you: going home.**

- If your phone has a round button below the screen, press it.
- If it has no button, swipe up from the very bottom edge of the screen in one smooth movement.

Lost? Strange screen? Something flashing? **Go home.** Nothing is lost or broken — every app is still there, waiting on the home screen.

---

## Opening and "closing" apps

1. Find the clock or weather app on your home screen.
2. Tap it once and wait a moment — apps can take a second or two.
3. Look around. Swipe up and down if there is more to see.
4. Go home (the rescue move above).

That is the entire rhythm of using a phone: **go in with a tap, come home with the home move.** You never need to switch anything off; going home is enough, and the phone tidies up after itself.

---

## Try it yourself

1. Go home. Open the camera app. Take a photo of anything.
2. Go home. Open the photos/gallery app and find your photo.
3. Go home. Swipe left, then right, and return to your first screen.
4. Press and hold an empty spot on the home screen — look at the menu that appears — then tap an empty space to dismiss it.

Do the whole list twice. The second time will feel easier. That feeling is the lesson.

---

## One-page summary (print me)

- Tap = choose. Swipe = turn pages. Press-and-hold = extra choices.
- The home screen is your front page; each picture is an app.
- Lost? **Go home**: round button, or swipe up from the bottom edge.
- Touching cannot break anything. Confusion is normal; home fixes it.
